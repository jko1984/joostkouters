CISSP

Certified Information Systems Security Professional. A broad, widely recognized credential covering security leadership and management.

CISM

Certified Information Security Manager. Focused on governance and running a security program, rather than hands-on technical work.

CRISC

Certified in Risk and Information Systems Control. Focused on identifying and managing IT risk.

CISA

Certified Information Systems Auditor. A standard credential for IT auditing and controls.

AAISM

Advanced in AI Security Management, a new ISACA certification for governing AI risk. I'm among the first people certified worldwide.

A bit about me, and what this isn't

I'm the only person doing security and privacy at a US AI company, reporting straight to the CFO. SOC 2 and ISO 27001 audits, board reporting, AI security architecture, customer contract reviews, it all lands on me. That's the day-to-day for a lot of security leaders outside the big tech companies, small team, broad scope, and it's probably closer to your situation than most of the advice out there.

I hold CISSP, CISM, CRISC and CISA, and picked up AAISM this year (details on each are in the badges above). None of that makes me a therapist, and I won't pretend it does. If you're dealing with burnout or need clinical support, a professional is the better call. Where I can actually help is more practical: whether a negotiation ask is reasonable, why a board might not trust your reporting, or what a certification like AAISM actually signals to an employer.

How this works in practice

We start with a free 30-minute call, mostly to figure out together whether I can actually help with what you're dealing with. If it's a fit, we do either one-off sessions or a short run of them built around something specific: an upcoming interview, a negotiation, a move into management, a first board presentation.
