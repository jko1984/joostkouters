A bit about me, and what this isn't

I'm the only person doing security and privacy at a US AI company, reporting straight to the CFO, no team of specialists to lean on. SOC 2 and ISO 27001 audits, board reporting, AI security architecture, customer contract reviews. All of it lands on me. That's not a complaint, it's just what the job looks like for most security leaders outside the big tech companies, and it's probably closer to your reality than what a Fortune 500 CISO would tell you.

I picked up CISSP, CISM, CRISC and CISA along the way, and this year I got AAISM, one of the first people anywhere to hold it. None of that makes me a therapist or a generic career coach, and I won't pretend it does. If you want help processing burnout or a personality assessment, I'm not your guy. If you want a straight answer on whether your negotiation ask is reasonable, why your board doesn't trust your reporting, or what AAISM actually signals to an employer, that's the conversation I'm good at.
